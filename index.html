LOOKING FOR A LATE-NIGHT DEAL? TIMING MAKES ALL THE DIFFERENCE.

By tracking after-dark activity through crime data, we can pinpoint the hours and places where the streets are busiest and when prices are likely lower.

WHEN TO GO

A well done heatmap is the best way to find out.

Hours are starting from 8 AM to better center the peak activity. The cluster red zone reveal the clearest patterns at a glance.

A heatmap showing hourly prostitution incidents per day in the city of San Francisco for the years 2020-25. Public data from SF official sources. Mind that even if the count number of incidents are quite low, the same pattern is shown for other year ranges indicating the sample is representative.

The busiest hours are between 8 PM and midnight, with a clear peak at 11 PM. This matches known global patterns for nightlife and prostitution activity (see research). Interestingly, midweek shows higher peaks than the weekend, which may reflect changes in police patrols during weekends.

WHERE TO GO

I know it's a HARD topic, but what about a less heated map?

Let's look into detailed geographic information to localize the best places to find a good time.

An overlay of the prostitution crimes's GPS location on the map of San Francisco for the years 2003-08 and 2020-25. Each dot is a registered crime. The map is interactive and can be toggled in the top right to show the two different time periods.

We see both concentrated clusters and scattered activity, with clear hotspots in the Mission and Tenderloin districts.

Interestingly, similar hotspots appear in the 2003-08 data, even though overall counts for this category have dropped sharply in recent years.

Some areas that were hot in 2003-08 now report almost no incidents, which could reflect actual changes in behavior, or yet another shift in police activity.

Conclusion

Even as overall incidents decline, the Mission and Tenderloin remain hotspots, with activity peaking between 8 PM and midnight. Midweek can be busier than weekends, highlighting subtle patterns in street behavior. Timing and location clearly matter, and understanding these trends can guide both analysis and policy decisions.
